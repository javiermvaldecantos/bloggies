Plato (428/427 or 424/423 BCE; 348/347 BCE) was a philosopher,
as well as mathematician, in Classical Greece, and an influential
figure in philosophy, central in Western philosophy.

He was Socrates' student, and founded the Academy in Athens, the first
institution of higher learning in the Western world.

Along with Socrates and his most famous student, Aristotle, Plato helped
to lay the foundations of Western philosophy and science.
