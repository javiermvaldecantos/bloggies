Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a species in the onion genus, Allium. 

Its close relatives include the onion, shallot, leek, chive, and rakkyo. 

With a history of human use of over 7,000 years, garlic is native to central Asia, 
and has long been a staple in the Mediterranean region, as well as a frequent 
seasoning in Asia, Africa, and Europe. 

It was known to Ancient Egyptians, and has been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
